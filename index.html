Winston Churchill

The British Bulldog.

He defeated the Nazis and warned against the Iron Curtain - Churchill remains one of the most influential people of the last century.

A Storied Career...

Prime Minister of the UK... Twice

Churchill was politically active for most of his adult life. He eventually achieved the highest elected position in the British government - Prime Minister. From this seat, he used his power to rearm the British and fight against the Nazis.

Won the War Against the Axis Powers

Churchill rallied the British people and returned from the brink of defeat to defeat the German war machine.

Warned Against the Impending Rise of the Soviet Empire

The "Iron Curtain" speech showed that Churchill was cognizant of the rising threat of the Eastern Block, and he attempted to warn everyone who would listen.

A Successful Statesman

He Was Elected to the Highest Elected Position of the UK Government

Churchill was not universally liked, but his ability to orate and garner feelings in the general public allowed him to rise to the highest elected position in the British Government.

He first served as Prime Minister from May 1940 until June 1945, and then again from October 1951 until April 1955.

By the end of his second term, he was all but forced out of office due to his declining health and his overbearing personality. However, despite this, he cemented the relationship between the United States and the UK, and ushered in an era of peace.

Kept Morale High against the German War Machine

Britain was on the brink of defeat against Germany, but Churchill was able to continually rally his government and subjects to support the war efforts and follow his plans.

The Wartime Leader that the UK Needed

Brokered The Lend Lease Agreement

The Lend Lease Agreement allowed Britain to rearm with American-made weapons and put up a fight against the Nazis in the early war.

Helped Shape the Allies' War Strategy

Churchill contributed to overall military posture of the Allies, and although all of his ideas did not come to fruition, he was able to steer the Allies into a unified front to confront the Axis powers.

Read more about Churchill's military career.

Predicted the Future of the Red Menace

Predicted the Expansionist Threat of the Soviet Union

As the war subsided and the Allied coalition began to fall apart, Churchill warned the West of the Soviet Union's goals to take over Eastern Europe.

He gave the famous "Iron Curtain" speech, which became a household topic, along with communism, Stalin, and Russia, especially as the Cold War began progressing.
